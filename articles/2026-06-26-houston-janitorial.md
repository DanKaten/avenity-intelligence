## Overview

We analyzed 10 janitorial and commercial cleaning businesses in Houston, Texas to assess their current AI search visibility — specifically, their ability to appear when potential customers ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews questions like *"who is the best janitorial company in Houston?"*

## Scoring Methodology

Each business is assessed across four technical dimensions using publicly observable signals only:

| Dimension | Max Points | What We Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Entity Clarity | 25 | Clear business description, service definition, location signals on website |
| Schema & Structure | 25 | Structured data markup, NAP consistency across platforms, technical signals |
| Authority & Reviews | 25 | Review volume and recency on publicly accessible platforms |
| Content Depth | 25 | Service pages, FAQ content, location pages, AI-answerable content |

**Score guide:** Strong (85–100) · Good (70–84) · Developing+ (55–69) · Developing (40–54) · Early Stage (below 40)

Scores reflect AI search visibility readiness only. A business with a low AI visibility score may be highly successful, well-regarded, and excellent at its trade — these scores measure one specific technical dimension of digital presence.

## Houston Janitorial AI Visibility Findings

### 1. PJS of Houston
**Website:** pjsofhouston.com | **AI Visibility Score: Developing+ (64/100)**

PJS of Houston provides commercial janitorial and office cleaning services across the Houston metro. In this analysis, PJS of Houston scored 64 out of 100.

**Technical findings:**
- Entity Clarity (16/25): The homepage opens with a facility-focused value statement. The primary service category and city are referenced within the first screen, though the H1 leads with a brand message rather than a direct service + location statement, which slightly reduces AI engine clarity.
- Schema & Structure (18/25): LocalBusiness schema markup was detected in the page source. Service-level structured data was not found on individual service pages, which limits AI engines' ability to categorize specific offerings.
- Authority & Reviews (16/25): Public review presence was not surfaced directly on the homepage. Third-party platform links appear in the site's footer area, though review count data is not prominently displayed in machine-readable form.
- Content Depth (14/25): Service category pages are present. FAQ-format content addressing common buyer questions — pricing, frequency, facility types served — was not detected, which is a signal AI engines use to generate direct answers.

**Highest-impact opportunity:** Adding a structured FAQ section to the main services page would meaningfully improve the site's ability to appear in AI-generated answers to buyer questions.
