# Texas AI Visibility Report 2026

## 800 Texas Service Businesses Scored on AI Search Visibility

*Avenity Intelligence &nbsp;·&nbsp; Published June 2026 &nbsp;·&nbsp; 8 Cities &nbsp;·&nbsp; 10 Industries*

---

When a homeowner asks ChatGPT *"who's the best HVAC company in Houston?"* or a property manager asks Perplexity *"recommend a reliable roofing contractor in Dallas"* — AI systems don't search the web the way Google does. They pull from a specific set of structured signals to decide which businesses to name, and which to ignore entirely.

We spent 30 days analyzing **800 Texas service businesses** across 8 cities and 10 industries to answer one question: who's visible in AI search, and what separates them from everyone else?

**The findings are stark.** The average score across all 800 businesses was **52 out of 100.** The majority of established Texas service businesses — companies with years of experience, real reviews, and genuine expertise — are effectively invisible to the AI systems now fielding millions of local buyer queries every day.

---

## What We Measured

Every business received a score from 0–100 across four dimensions:

**Entity Clarity (25 pts)** — Does AI know exactly what your business does, where you operate, and who you serve? Clear title tags, prominent H1 headings, and geographic anchoring tell AI systems how to categorize and recall your business when a buyer asks.

**Schema & Structure (25 pts)** — Does your website speak the technical language AI reads? JSON-LD structured data — LocalBusiness schema, service definitions, NAP consistency — gives AI the machine-readable signals it needs to confidently recommend you.

**Authority & Reviews (25 pts)** — Do third parties confirm your expertise? Review volume, ratings signals, testimonials, and external citations tell AI systems you're a real, trusted business worth mentioning to a buyer who's ready to hire.

**Content Depth (25 pts)** — Do you answer the questions buyers actually ask? FAQ content, detailed service pages, and project galleries give AI the raw material to cite you when a buyer's question matches your expertise.

| Score Range | Label | Share of Businesses |
|---|---|---|
| 85–100 | Strong | 4% |
| 70–84 | Good | 12% |

| 40–54 | Developing | 38% |
| Below 40 | Early Stage | 23% |

**96% of businesses score below 85.** Even businesses with strong Google Maps presence and hundreds of positive reviews frequently score below 60 due to missing schema markup and thin structured content — two gaps AI systems penalize heavily.

---

## The 3 Most Common Gaps

These issues appeared in the majority of businesses analyzed:

1. **No schema markup** — 67% of businesses had no JSON-LD structured data on their homepage. AI systems can't confirm your name, address, services, or credentials without it.
2. **Missing geographic signals** — 54% of businesses had no city or service-area language in their primary heading structure. AI can't place you locally without explicit signals.
3. **No FAQ content** — 71% of businesses had no FAQ section. Buyers are asking AI comparison and process questions; businesses without FAQ content never enter those conversations.
